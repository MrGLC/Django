All About Thyme: A Fragrant and Beneficial Herb

What does Thyme look like?
  • Small, woody perennial shrub with a spreading or upright growth habit.
  • Tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green or gray-green.
  • Clusters of delicate pink, white, or lavender flowers in summer.
Why should I plant Thyme?
  • Culinary delight: Adds unique flavor to many dishes.
  • Attracts pollinators: Bees and butterflies love its blooms.
  • Low-maintenance: Tolerates drought and poor soil conditions.
  • Aromatic and medicinal: Used in aromatherapy and traditional remedies.
Interesting facts about Thyme:
  • Belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae).
  • Ancient Egyptians used thyme for embalming.
  • Greek soldiers bathed in thyme water for courage.
  • There are over 350 different thyme varieties.
How do I germinate Thyme seeds?

  • Keep soil moist but not waterlogged.

Additional Tips:
  • Plant thyme in full sun and well-drained soil.
  • Harvest frequently to promote bushy growth.
  • Dry or freeze thyme leaves to preserve its flavor.
